Why Physical AI

Physical AI is the next distribution layer of intelligence

— and one of the largest opportunities of the coming decade.

AI has scaled rapidly in the digital world. The physical world is only beginning.

The real world provides elements that pure software cannot:

Rich sensory data · Contextual feedback · Physical constraints

These forces push AI models and agents to evolve faster and become more robust.

Physical AI includes:

AI Devices

Standalone hardware powered by on-device intelligence.

Wearables & Sensors

Always-on data streams from the body and environment.

Embodied Systems

Robotics and intelligent machines acting in the real world.

FOUNTAIN 2

The Real-World Gap

There is still a large gap between a compelling AI demo and a real-world product that can ship at scale.

Building ≠ Shipping

Building models or applications is not the same as shipping physical systems.

Prototype → Mass Production

Moving from prototype to mass production requires:

  • System integration
  • Supply chain capability
  • Manufacturing expertise
  • Rapid iteration cycles

Access to Asia

US-based teams often need Shenzhen and Asia to access:

  • Factories & components
  • Manufacturing partners
  • Production iteration speed

Ship or Stay Demo

Great models are necessary but not sufficient. Real-world systems must be designed, validated, manufactured, shipped. Otherwise they remain demos.
